Assignment 1: Interactive Experience Research Project

https://www.myoungaekim.com/

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to?

The first thing I noticed was the stark black and white interface, where only a few moving images broke the silence. Videos autoplayed, and there was no obvious interactivity at first glance.

2. List of actions in two minutes:

  • Clicked on box 1: Viewed the content
  • Clicked on box 2: Explored the revealed project
  • Clicked on box 3: Observed the displayed work
  • Clicked on box 4: Engaged with the content inside

3. Most time engaging with?

Clicking on each window took most of my attention — each one revealed new content with different styles and arrangements.

4. Most common action?

Scrolling and clicking windows. But the harsh contrast and lack of rounded borders made the site feel visually rigid.

5. Impression of the goal?

It feels like a digital showcase of creative exploration, presented through a gamified calendar interface.

6. How is this goal communicated?

The numbered boxes and hidden surprises suggest discovery and creative depth — each click unveils something new.

7. Interaction over time?

First-time users might leave early, but deeper exploration encourages extended visits as more is revealed.

8. How is this communicated?

The calendar-style layout implies a finite number of items to be completed in one go — or revisited casually.

9. Media references?

Advent calendars and digital portfolios are both clearly referenced.

10. What does this tell you about interaction?

It suggests exploration should be slow and methodical — box-by-box, like opening gifts.

11. What about how you should feel?

Anticipation and delight are embedded in the interface — you’re meant to enjoy each step.

12. Most frustrating part?

The lack of color variety and hover cues made it feel flat and uncertain at first.

13. Most satisfying part?

The moment when a window opens to reveal something unexpected — it’s satisfying and keeps you curious.
